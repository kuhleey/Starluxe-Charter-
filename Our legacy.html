Our Historical Timeline

From 1956 to present day

The Humble Beginnings (1956)

1956

Our journey started in Johannesburg when the grandfather and father of two current Directors began operating a service between Johannesburg and Vereeniging - our first inter-city route.

David Bus Services (1958)

1958

We purchased a small company based in Springs called David Bus Services. The original partnership documents show Japie Nyakale entering an agreement with Suliman Mahomed Moolla for permits to operate two routes from Springs.

Family Leadership (1963)

1963

After losing the family patriarch, the business was taken over by brothers Ismail, Mahomed, Ahmad and Abdul Hamid. They expanded routes from Springs despite the challenges of operating as people of color during apartheid.

Birth of Eldo Coaches (1988)

1988

Under CC Moolla's Transport Services, we became the first black company awarded a government subsidized contract to operate routes from Eldorado Park to major industrial hubs around Johannesburg. The name "Eldo" paid tribute to the community we served.

Golden Wheels Acquisition (1995)

1995

We purchased Broadway Bus Lines CC, trading as Golden Wheels, which was the only operator servicing the Lenasia to Durban route. This marked our expansion into new territory with an initial fleet of 5 coaches and 2 minibuses.

Embracing Innovation(2006)

2006

In 2006, the younger generation of the family assumed greater leadership roles within the business. They introduced fresh perspectives, mordernized operations, and aligned strategies with evolving market trends ushering in an innovative new phase of growth for the company.

Corporate Restructuring (2011)

2011

The company was restructured as David Bus Services (Pty) Ltd trading as Eldo Coaches, with Ahmad Moolla, Mohsin Moolla and Rashida Bana as directors. The name honored our origins while looking to the future.

National Expansion (2011-2023)

2011-2023

Under Mohsin Moolla's leadership, we grew from 15 vehicles and 50 employees to 65 vehicles and 280+ staff nationwide. We opened branches across South Africa including Cape Town (2013) and expanded our executive touring division.

Eldo Coaches Closure (2023)

2023

After 67 years of service, Eldo Coaches ceased operations due to economic challenges and industry pressures. The closure marked the end of an era for this iconic South African transport company.

Starluxe Rebrand (2025)

2025

Rising from the legacy of Eldo Coaches, the company rebranded as Starluxe, bringing new energy and vision to South African transport while honoring the Moolla family's decades of service to the industry.
